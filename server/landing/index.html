Why this exists

Every major transcription service uploads your audio to their servers. Your medical notes. Legal consultations. Therapy sessions. Business meetings.

They say they delete it. Some probably do. But why risk it?

We built this because we needed transcription we could trust. Everything runs locally using WebAssembly and OpenAI's Whisper model.

How it works

01

Upload or record

Drop an audio file or record directly. Your mic data never leaves the browser.

02

AI processes locally

Whisper AI runs in your browser via WebAssembly. Zero network requests.

03

Done

Read, edit, export, or delete your transcription. It's yours.

Technical note: First use downloads the Whisper model (~75MB). After that, everything works offline. You can verify zero network activity during transcription by opening DevTools.

Questions

Is it actually free?
Yes. No trial, no credit card, no bullshit. Since everything runs in your browser, we have no server costs.
How good is the transcription?
We use OpenAI's Whisper model - the same tech powering most paid services. Accuracy is comparable to anything you'd pay for.
What audio formats work?
MP3, WAV, M4A, OGG, and most common formats. You can also record directly in the browser.
Does it work offline?
After the first use (when it downloads the AI model), yes. Fully offline.
Where's my data stored?
In your browser's IndexedDB. You can export or delete it anytime. We literally can't access it.
Can I self-host it?
Yes. It's open source. Clone the repo, run it anywhere.
What about mobile?
Works on mobile browsers, but processing is slower on phones. Best on desktop.
How do you make money?
We don't. This is a tool we built for ourselves and decided to share. Maybe we'll add paid features later, but the core will always be free.
